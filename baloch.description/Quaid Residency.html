Quaid-e-Azam Residency

The historic wooden residency in Ziarat where Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah spent his final days.

Introduction

The Quaid-e-Azam Residency, commonly known as **Ziarat Residency**, is a national heritage site located in Ziarat, Balochistan. This wooden structure, surrounded by ancient juniper forests, holds immense historical significance as it served as the residence of Pakistan’s founder, Muhammad Ali Jinnah, during the last months of his life in 1948.

Today, it stands as a symbol of Pakistan’s independence struggle and is one of the most visited historical landmarks in the country.

History

  • 1892: Originally constructed by the British as a sanatorium.
  • 1948: Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah spent the last 2 months of his life here.
  • 1970s: Declared a national monument due to its historic importance.
  • 2013: Severely damaged in a terrorist attack but later fully restored to its original form.

Tourism

Quaid-e-Azam Residency is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Balochistan. Visitors are drawn to its colonial architecture, peaceful environment, and historical value. The surrounding juniper forests and the cool climate of Ziarat make it an ideal year-round travel spot.

  • Historic building with preserved belongings of Quaid-e-Azam.
  • Guided tours available for visitors.
  • Photography spot with panoramic views of Ziarat valley.
  • Nearby attractions include Prospect Point and Ziarat Juniper Forests.
