Hingol National Park

The largest national park of Pakistan, home to mountains, desert, rivers, and wildlife.

Introduction

**Hingol National Park** is the largest national park in Pakistan, located along the Makran Coastal Highway in Balochistan. Covering an area of over 6,000 square kilometers, the park is famous for its unique landscapes including rugged mountains, deserts, and the Arabian Sea coastline.

The park is home to a rich variety of wildlife such as the Sindh ibex, urial, chinkara, and migratory birds. It also houses natural wonders like the **Princess of Hope** rock formation and cultural landmarks like **Hinglaj Mata Temple**.

Highlights of Hingol National Park

  • Area: Covers more than 6,000 sq km.
  • Wildlife: Home to ibex, urial, chinkara, crocodiles, and migratory birds.
  • Geography: Mixture of desert, river, mountains, and coastal areas.
  • Landmarks: Princess of Hope, Lion of Balochistan, Hinglaj Mata Temple.
  • Tourism: Popular for camping, road trips, and nature exploration.

Tourism

Hingol National Park attracts nature lovers, adventurers, and spiritual visitors alike. The blend of desert, mountains, and sea makes it one of the most unique destinations in Pakistan.

  • Visit the Princess of Hope and Lion of Balochistan rock formations.
  • Explore Hinglaj Mata Temple, an important Hindu pilgrimage site.
  • Enjoy camping and stargazing in the desert surroundings.
  • Spot rare wildlife and migratory birds.
  • Take a road trip along the Makran Coastal Highway.
