Mud Volcanoes

Natural mud volcanoes located near Hingol National Park, Balochistan.

Introduction

The **Mud Volcanoes of Balochistan** are rare natural wonders located near **Hingol National Park** along the Makran Coastal Highway. Unlike lava volcanoes, these are formed when gases and underground mud push to the surface, creating cone-shaped mud mounds.

Pakistan is home to more than **80 mud volcanoes**, with several clustered in this region. The most famous among them are **Chandragupt Mud Volcano** and **Malanji Mud Volcano**, which also hold religious significance for Hindu pilgrims visiting Hinglaj Mata Temple.

Key Features

  • Location: Near Hingol National Park, Makran Coastal Highway, Balochistan.
  • Type: Mud volcanoes formed by methane and gas pressure.
  • Height: Some cones rise up to 100 feet above ground.
  • Religious Importance: Considered sacred by Hindu pilgrims of Hinglaj Mata Temple.
  • Unique Fact: They are cold mud volcanoes, not fiery like lava volcanoes.

Tourism

The Mud Volcanoes attract curious travelers, scientists, and Hindu pilgrims alike. They are often visited as part of tours along the **Makran Coastal Highway**.

  • Hiking to the top of mud volcano cones.
  • Photography of unique mud landscapes.
  • Combined tours with Kund Malir Beach & Hingol National Park.
  • Pilgrimage rituals by Hindu devotees.
