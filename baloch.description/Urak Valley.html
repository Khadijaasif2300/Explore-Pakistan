Hanna Lake

A scenic lake near Quetta surrounded by rugged hills and pine trees.

Introduction

Hanna Lake is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Balochistan, located about 14 kilometers east of Quetta. Surrounded by rugged mountains and pine trees, the lake offers a peaceful environment and scenic beauty that attracts visitors throughout the year. The turquoise waters of the lake make it one of the most photographed sites in the region.

The lake is a perfect spot for picnics, boating, and enjoying nature. A lakeside restaurant and huts are available for visitors to relax while taking in the breathtaking views.

History

  • 1894: Hanna Lake was constructed during the British colonial era to control floodwaters coming from the Zarghoon and Murree mountain streams.
  • 20th Century: Became a popular recreational spot for locals and tourists alike.
  • Recent Years: Faced periods of drought, leading to low water levels, but efforts have been made to restore its beauty.
