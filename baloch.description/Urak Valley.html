Urak Valley

The "Valley of Orchards" located near Quetta, famous for apple and cherry gardens.

Introduction

Urak Valley, located about 21 kilometers from Quetta, is often called the "Valley of Orchards" because of its numerous fruit gardens. The valley is surrounded by lush green trees, mountains, and streams, making it a favorite picnic spot for locals and tourists alike.

The valley is especially famous for its apple, apricot, and cherry orchards, which bloom beautifully in spring and summer, creating a picturesque landscape.

History

  • Ancient Times: Known for its natural water streams and fertile lands that made it ideal for farming.
  • British Era: The valley was developed further and gained popularity as a summer retreat.
  • Present Day: Urak Valley remains a key tourist attraction and a vital source of fruits for Balochistan.

Attractions and Tourism

Urak Valley is a popular tourist destination for families and nature lovers. Key attractions include:

  • Fruit orchards, especially apples and cherries.
  • Fresh water streams and springs.
  • Nearby Hanna Lake and Wali Tangi Dam.
  • Picnic spots and walking trails surrounded by nature.
