Astola Island

Pakistan’s largest offshore island, known for its unique beauty and biodiversity.

Introduction

Astola Island, also called "Island of the Seven Hills," is located in the Arabian Sea near Pasni, Balochistan. It is Pakistan’s largest offshore island, stretching about 7 km in length and 2.5 km in width.

The island is uninhabited and famous for its unique ecosystem, crystal-clear waters, and rocky cliffs. It was declared a marine protected area in 2017, making it an important site for nature and adventure lovers.

Key Attractions

  • Scenic Beauty: Rugged cliffs, sandy beaches, and turquoise waters.
  • Marine Life: Habitat for turtles, dolphins, and rare coral reefs.
  • Adventure: Ideal for camping, snorkeling, and scuba diving.
  • Cultural Significance: Remnants of an ancient Hindu temple and a mosque exist on the island.

Tourism

Astola Island is a hidden paradise for adventurers and nature enthusiasts. Though remote, it attracts tourists for camping, scuba diving, fishing, and exploring marine life. Since there are no permanent facilities, visitors bring their own supplies.

  • Camping under the open sky.
  • Snorkeling and scuba diving.
  • Boating and fishing trips.
  • Exploring ancient ruins and natural caves.
