Pasni Beach

A serene stretch of the Makran Coast near the port town of Pasni in Gwadar District, Balochistan.

Introduction

Pasni Beach lies along the Arabian Sea beside the fishing port of Pasni, about 135 km east of Gwadar on the Makran Coastal Highway. Known for its quiet coves, sandy stretches and rocky headlands, the area offers calm seascapes, local fishing culture and coastal birdlife.

The town’s economy revolves around fishing and small-scale ship repair, and the beach is a great place to watch traditional wooden boats (dhows), sunrise/sunset hues, and migratory seabirds.

Highlights

  • Quiet Shores: Wide sandy sections punctuated by rocky promontories and tidal pools.
  • Fishing Harbor: See colorful boats, nets and lively fish auctions (mornings).
  • Wildlife: Coastal birds, occasional dolphins offshore; best with binoculars.
  • Scenic Drives: Makran Coastal Highway viewpoints toward Shadi Kaur and Ras Jaddi.
  • Local Culture: Friendly fishing community and fresh seafood eateries.

Activities & Visitor Tips

  • Beach walks, shoreline photography and sunrise/sunset viewing.
  • Boat-watching at the harbor; request permission before photographing people.
  • Pack-in/pack-out: limited facilities directly on the beach—carry water and snacks.
  • Best time: October–March for milder weather and clearer skies.
  • Safety: Observe tides and avoid isolated cliffs during strong winds or waves.

Getting There

  • Road: On the Makran Coastal Highway (N-10); Pasni lies east of Gwadar and west of Ormara.
  • In Town: Short drives or tuk-tuks from Pasni bazaar to beach spots and the harbor.
  • Services: Basic guesthouses and eateries in Pasni; more options in Gwadar/Ormara.
