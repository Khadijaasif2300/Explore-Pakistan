Ziarat Residency

The historic wooden residency where Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah spent his last days.

Introduction

Ziarat is a scenic hill station in Balochistan, Pakistan, located at an altitude of about 8,000 feet. It is renowned for its pleasant climate, centuries-old juniper forests, and historical importance.

The most famous landmark here is the **Quaid-e-Azam Residency**, where the founder of Pakistan, Muhammad Ali Jinnah, spent the last months of his life in 1948.

History

  • 1890s: Ziarat Residency was built during the British colonial era as a sanatorium.
  • 1948: Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah spent his final days here, making it a national heritage site.
  • Present: The residency has been restored after a fire incident in 2013 and is now a major tourist and historical attraction.

Attractions and Tourism

Ziarat is famous not only for its historic Residency but also for its natural beauty. Popular attractions include:

  • Quaid-e-Azam Residency (Ziarat Residency).
  • Ancient Juniper Forests – some trees are over 5,000 years old.
  • Prospect Point – a viewpoint overlooking the valley.
  • Snowfall during winter, making Ziarat a winter wonderland.
