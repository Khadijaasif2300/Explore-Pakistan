Shandur Pass

The "Roof of the World" and home of the world’s highest polo ground in northern Pakistan.

Introduction

Shandur Pass is a high mountain pass located at an elevation of approximately 3,700 meters (12,139 feet) in the Ghizer District of Gilgit-Baltistan. It connects Chitral with Gilgit-Baltistan and is renowned for its open meadows, stunning alpine scenery, and cultural significance.

The pass is famous for hosting the annual Shandur Polo Festival, where teams from Chitral and Gilgit compete in polo matches on the world’s highest polo ground, attracting tourists and sports enthusiasts from across Pakistan.

History & Background

  • Traditional Route: Historically, Shandur Pass was used as a trade and travel route between Chitral and Gilgit regions.
  • Polo Tradition: Polo has been played at Shandur Pass for centuries, with modern annual festivals organized since the mid-20th century.
  • Tourism: The scenic meadows and cultural events attract national and international visitors, particularly in July when the festival is held.

Attractions & Activities

  • Watching or participating in the Shandur Polo Festival (usually in July).
  • Trekking through alpine meadows and nearby mountains.
  • Photography of panoramic landscapes, wildlife, and cultural events.
  • Camping in designated areas with spectacular sunrise and sunset views.
  • Exploring local culture and interacting with Chitrali and Gilgiti communities.

Visitor Information

  • Best Time to Visit: June to September; July for the Shandur Polo Festival.
  • Access: Reachable by road from Chitral or Gilgit; mountain roads can be narrow and steep, suitable for 4×4 vehicles.
  • Accommodation: Temporary tents and basic guesthouses; facilities are limited, so come prepared.
  • Altitude Precautions: Carry warm clothing, and acclimatize for high-altitude conditions.
  • Respect Local Customs: Follow guidance of local organizers during polo festival and community areas.
