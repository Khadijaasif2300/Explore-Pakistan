Peshawar City

The capital of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan, rich in history, culture, and architecture.

Introduction

Peshawar is one of the oldest cities in South Asia, serving as the capital of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. The city has historically been a cultural, political, and economic hub of the region, located near the historic Khyber Pass.

Known for its bazaars, traditional cuisine, and historic landmarks, Peshawar is a blend of modern urban life and centuries-old heritage. The city has been influenced by various empires, including the Gandhara, Mughal, Durrani, and British.

History & Culture

  • Ancient Era: Part of Gandhara civilization with Buddhist stupas and monasteries.
  • Mughal Period: Became a significant trade and administrative center.
  • Durrani and Sikh Periods: Further developed forts, gardens, and cultural landmarks.
  • British Era: Modernized infrastructure, roads, and educational institutions.
  • Modern Era: Capital of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa; vibrant culture, tourism, and commerce hub.

Main Attractions & Activities

  • Bala Hisar Fort: Historical fort with panoramic city views.
  • Qissa Khwani Bazaar: Traditional market famous for crafts and food.
  • Mahabat Khan Mosque: Iconic 17th-century mosque.
  • Islamia College Peshawar: Historic college with unique architecture.
  • Peshawar Museum: Houses Gandhara art and artifacts.
  • Local cuisine and cultural festivals: Taste traditional foods and attend cultural events.

Visitor Information

  • Best Time to Visit: October to March for pleasant weather.
  • Access: Well-connected by road, air, and rail; Peshawar International Airport serves the city.
  • Accommodation: Hotels, guesthouses, and hostels available in central areas.
  • Guidelines: Respect local customs and dress modestly; guided tours recommended for historical sites.
  • Photography: Allowed in public areas; seek permission in religious or private places.
