Kumrat Valley

A picturesque valley in Upper Dir District, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, known for rivers, dense forests, and alpine meadows.

Introduction

Kumrat Valley is located in the Upper Dir District of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan. Nestled in the Hindu Raj mountain range, the valley is celebrated for its dense coniferous forests, flowing rivers, and green alpine meadows. It is one of the emerging tourist destinations in northern Pakistan, offering peace, natural beauty, and opportunities for outdoor adventure.

The Panjkora River flows through the valley, providing a scenic and tranquil environment. Kumrat Valley remains relatively untouched compared to other northern tourist spots, making it ideal for nature lovers and eco-tourists.

Main Attractions

  • Lush green meadows surrounded by mountains.
  • Dense forests of pine, deodar, and spruce.
  • Panjkora River offering scenic beauty and fishing opportunities.
  • Local wildlife such as ibex, snow leopards (rare), and various birds.
  • Traditional villages showcasing local culture and hospitality.
  • Nearby trekking routes and mountain trails.

Activities

  • Trekking and hiking in surrounding hills.
  • Camping along rivers and in alpine meadows.
  • Nature photography and wildlife observation.
  • Fishing in Panjkora River (where permitted).
  • Interacting with local communities and experiencing cultural traditions.
