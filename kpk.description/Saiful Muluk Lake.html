Saiful Muluk Lake

A high-altitude alpine lake in the Kaghan Valley, famed for its clear waters and fairy-tale legends.

Introduction

Saiful Muluk Lake is an alpine glacial lake situated near the town of Naran in the Kaghan Valley, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan. Nestled at an elevation of about 3,224 meters (10,578 ft), the lake is famous for its crystal-clear water, surrounding dramatic peaks (including Malika Parbat), and local folktales.

The lake is named after the legendary prince Saif ul Malook, whose romantic tale with the fairy princess Badi-ul-Jamal is immortalized in Sufi poetry by the poet Mian Muhammad Bakhsh.

History & Legend

  • Natural origin: Formed by glacial activity in the upper Kaghan Valley.
  • Local legend: The romantic epic of Prince Saif ul Malook and the fairy princess is a central cultural story associated with the lake and region.
  • Tourism growth: Became widely visited in the 20th and 21st centuries as road access to Naran improved.

Activities & Visitor Info

Saiful Muluk is a popular spot for nature lovers, photographers, and trekkers. Common activities include:

  • Shikara/boat rides (seasonal, when the lake is not frozen).
  • Short hikes and picnics around the lakeshore.
  • Photography of reflections, wildlife and alpine scenery.
  • Camping in nearby meadows (with proper permission and weather awareness).

Access: Reachable from Naran by jeep/4×4 during summer; road access may be closed in heavy winter due to snow. Best visited between May and September for easiest access.

Practical Tips

  • Altitude: The lake is high-altitude—carry warm clothing and be aware of altitude sickness symptoms.
  • Facilities: Basic facilities in Naran; limited services at the lake—bring water, snacks, and sun protection.
  • Respect local customs and natural environment; avoid littering and stay on marked paths.
  • Weather can change rapidly—check local conditions before visiting.
