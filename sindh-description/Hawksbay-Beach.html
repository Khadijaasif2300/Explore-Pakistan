Hawksbay Beach

A serene and popular beach of Karachi, known for its golden sands and Arabian Sea waves.

Introduction

Hawksbay Beach is one of the most famous beaches near Karachi, located along the Arabian Sea. It is a favorite spot for families, tourists, and picnickers due to its clean sandy shores, calm environment, and accessibility from the city.

The beach is also well known for its turtle population, as endangered green sea turtles come here to lay eggs during the nesting season, making it an important ecological site.

History

  • Colonial Period: Hawksbay was named after a British official and was once a remote picnic spot for elite visitors of Karachi.
  • Post-Independence: The beach became popular among Karachi residents for recreation and seaside huts.
  • Present Day: It remains a hub for tourism, turtle conservation, and eco-tourism activities.

Attractions & Tourism

Hawksbay is known for its beach huts, horse and camel rides, and water sports. Visitors enjoy sunrise and sunset views, swimming, and traditional seafood.

The WWF and Sindh Wildlife Department also run turtle conservation programs here, making it an important spot for eco-tourism and wildlife awareness.
