Noor Mahal

A royal palace in Bahawalpur, built in the Italian-style architecture.

Introduction

Noor Mahal, located in Bahawalpur, Punjab, Pakistan, is a 19th-century palace built by the Nawab of Bahawalpur. It is one of the finest examples of Italian-style architecture in the region. The palace was constructed as a symbol of grandeur and luxury, reflecting the cultural richness of the Abbasi rulers. Today, it stands as a heritage site, attracting visitors from all over the country.

History

  • 1872: Noor Mahal was commissioned by Nawab Sadiq Muhammad Khan IV.
  • 1875: Completed as a palace designed for state functions and royal residence.
  • 20th Century: Palace used occasionally by the Nawabs, later came under government use.
  • 2001: Taken over by the Pakistan Army, which undertook restoration and preservation work.
  • Present: Maintained as a heritage monument, open to the public for tours.

Architecture

Noor Mahal is built in a neoclassical Italian style, with elements of subcontinental design. The palace covers an area of about 44,600 square feet and consists of 32 rooms, including 14 in the basement. Its design includes Corinthian columns, domes, and exquisite wooden doors and windows.

The interior features elegant halls, verandas, and intricate ceilings, decorated with chandeliers and European furnishings. The palace has a central dome, flanked by smaller domes, making it one of the most iconic landmarks in Bahawalpur.
