Murree Hills

The Queen of Hills – a lush green hill station known for its natural beauty, colonial heritage, and cool climate.

Introduction

Murree, often called the “Queen of Hills,” is a famous hill station located in the Pir Panjal range of the Himalayas, around 60 kilometers northeast of Islamabad in Punjab, Pakistan. At an altitude of about 7,000 feet above sea level, Murree is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the country.

Known for its pine forests, snow-covered winters, and pleasant summers, Murree has long been a retreat for people seeking relief from the scorching plains of Punjab. It also carries historical significance as a British colonial-era hill station.

History

  • 1850s: Murree was founded by the British as a hill station and summer retreat.
  • 1851: It was established as the summer headquarters of the Punjab government during colonial rule.
  • Late 19th Century: Churches, schools, and colonial-style houses were built, giving Murree its distinct character.
  • Post-1947: After independence, Murree became a prime tourist destination for Pakistanis.
  • Present Day: Murree continues to attract millions of visitors each year for its cool climate, scenic views, and heritage.

Main Attractions

Murree offers a blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Its main attractions include:

  • Mall Road: The bustling hub of Murree with shops, cafes, and colonial-era buildings.
  • Pindi Point & Kashmir Point: Famous viewpoints offering breathtaking landscapes.
  • Patriata (New Murree): A modern hill station with chairlifts and panoramic views.
  • Ayubia & Nathia Gali: Nearby hill resorts rich in wildlife, hiking trails, and forests.
