Soon Valley

A breathtaking valley in the Salt Range, rich in natural beauty, history, and culture.

Introduction

Soon Valley, located in the Khushab District of Punjab, is one of the most picturesque regions of Pakistan. Nestled in the Salt Range, it stretches about 56 kilometers and is surrounded by rolling hills, fertile fields, and freshwater lakes. The valley is known for its natural beauty, unique wildlife, and a blend of cultural and historical sites.

It remains an important tourist destination, offering a mix of lush green landscapes, archaeological remains, and sacred shrines that highlight the valley’s significance in Punjab’s cultural heritage.

History

  • Ancient Times: The valley was historically part of various empires, including the Mauryan and Kushan, due to its location along trade routes.
  • Hindu & Buddhist Heritage: The area holds traces of temples and remains reflecting religious diversity before the advent of Islam.
  • Mughal Period: The Mughals admired Soon Valley for its beauty and used it as a retreat.
  • Modern Era: Today, the valley is famous for its lakes, gardens, and archaeological ruins, attracting researchers and tourists alike.

Main Attractions

Soon Valley offers a blend of natural wonders and historical landmarks:

  • Uchhali, Khabbeki, and Jahlar Lakes: Natural saltwater lakes, home to migratory birds and scenic beauty.
  • Akrand Fort: Historical ruins of a fort overlooking the valley.
  • Kanhatti Gardens: A botanical garden built during British rule, famous for citrus fruits.
  • Sakesar Peak: The highest peak in the Salt Range, offering panoramic views of the valley.
  • Sacred Shrines: The valley is home to Sufi shrines and historical sites of religious importance.
