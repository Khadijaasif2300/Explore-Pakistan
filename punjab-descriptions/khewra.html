Khewra Salt Mines

The second-largest salt mine in the world and one of Pakistan’s most unique heritage sites.

Introduction

The Khewra Salt Mines, located in the Punjab province near Pind Dadan Khan in the Jhelum District, are the largest and oldest salt mines in Pakistan, and the second-largest in the world. Situated at the foothills of the Salt Range, these mines are a major source of the famous pink Himalayan salt, exported worldwide.

Besides being an important source of natural mineral wealth, the mines have also become a major tourist attraction, drawing thousands of visitors every year for their fascinating underground tunnels, salt formations, and historical significance.

History

  • 326 BCE: The discovery of salt at Khewra is attributed to the soldiers of Alexander the Great, who noticed their horses licking salty rocks.
  • Mughal Era: Organized mining began under the Mughal Empire.
  • 1849: After the British annexed Punjab, systematic mining methods were introduced.
  • Present Day: The mines continue to produce about 387,000 tons of rock salt annually, supplying both local and international markets.

Main Attractions

The mines are not only a working source of salt but also a remarkable tourist site with unique features:

  • Salt Formations: Natural formations and sculptures carved from salt, including replicas of the Minar-e-Pakistan and Badshahi Mosque.
  • Salt Mosque: A small mosque made entirely of multicolored salt bricks inside the mine.
  • Salt Lakes: Underground pools with saline water reflecting the mine’s beauty.
  • Health Benefits: Certain chambers are used as therapy centers for patients with asthma and respiratory diseases.
