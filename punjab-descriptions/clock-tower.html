Faisalabad Clock Tower & Bazaars

The iconic centerpiece of Faisalabad, surrounded by historic bazaars shaped in the form of the Union Jack.

Introduction

The Faisalabad Clock Tower, locally known as “Ghanta Ghar,” is one of the oldest monuments of the city, built in 1903 during British rule. It stands at the center of the city and is surrounded by eight bazaars, laid out in the pattern of the British Union Jack flag.

These bazaars form the commercial heart of Faisalabad, famous for textiles, spices, jewelry, and traditional goods, making the area both a historical landmark and a bustling marketplace.

History

  • 1892: The foundation of the city (then called Lyallpur) was laid out on the design of British engineer Sir Ganga Ram.
  • 1903: The Clock Tower was completed under the supervision of British authorities, financed by local landlords (Zamindars).
  • Early 20th Century: The surrounding eight bazaars were established, radiating outward in the shape of the Union Jack.
  • Post-Independence: The tower and bazaars became symbols of Faisalabad’s identity as the “Manchester of Pakistan.”

Main Attractions

The Clock Tower and its surrounding bazaars form a unique cultural and commercial hub:

  • Eight Bazaars: Each bazaar is named after the goods traditionally sold, such as Katchery Bazaar, Jhang Bazaar, Aminpur Bazaar, and Rail Bazaar.
  • Traditional Shopping: Famous for textiles, spices, handicrafts, jewelry, and local street food.
  • Cultural Symbol: The Clock Tower is the heart of Faisalabad and a central meeting place for residents.
  • Festive Celebrations: National events and cultural gatherings often take place around the tower square.
