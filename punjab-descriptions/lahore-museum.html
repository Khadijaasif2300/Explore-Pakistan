Lahore Museum

The largest and most renowned museum in Pakistan, preserving centuries of history and culture.

Introduction

The Lahore Museum, established in 1865 during the British Raj, is located on The Mall, Lahore. Known locally as the "Wonder House," it is the largest museum in Pakistan and houses one of the most extensive collections of art, culture, and heritage in South Asia.

Its Indo-Saracenic style building, designed by Sir Ganga Ram, was completed in 1894. The museum preserves artifacts from the Indus Valley Civilization, Gandhara art, Mughal and Sikh periods, and the colonial era.

History

  • 1865: The museum was founded in a small building near Lahore’s Tollinton Market.
  • 1894: The current red-brick Indo-Saracenic style building was inaugurated.
  • Early 20th Century: Became one of the leading museums in South Asia, attracting scholars and researchers.
  • Present Day: Houses over 20 galleries with collections spanning thousands of years of history.

Main Attractions

The Lahore Museum is home to diverse collections that reflect the cultural and historical evolution of the region:

  • Gandhara Art: World-famous sculptures including the iconic Fasting Buddha.
  • Mughal & Sikh Artifacts: Coins, manuscripts, paintings, and weaponry from imperial eras.
  • Indus Valley Civilization: Ancient pottery, seals, and relics.
  • Islamic Gallery: Manuscripts of the Qur’an, Islamic calligraphy, and miniature paintings.
  • Colonial Period: Paintings, portraits, and relics of British rule in India.
  • Ethnological Gallery: Displays of Punjabi cultural heritage, crafts, and traditions.
