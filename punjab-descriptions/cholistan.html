Cholistan Desert

A vast desert in southern Punjab, rich in culture, forts, and traditions.

Introduction

The Cholistan Desert, locally known as Rohi, is located in Bahawalpur District, Punjab. Covering about 16,000 square kilometers, it extends into the Thar Desert of Sindh and Rajasthan (India). The desert is known for its golden sand dunes, nomadic lifestyle, ancient forts, and cultural heritage.

Despite its arid climate, Cholistan is home to historic trade routes, camel caravans, and centuries-old traditions that reflect the resilience and vibrancy of its people.

History

  • Ancient Period: The region was once watered by the Hakra River, which supported settlements of the Indus Valley Civilization.
  • Medieval Era: Served as a trade route connecting South Asia with Central Asia and Persia.
  • 15th–18th Century: Numerous forts, including Derawar Fort, were built to protect caravans and control trade.
  • Modern Era: Cholistan remains a cultural hub, famous for camel breeding, traditional crafts, and annual festivals.

Main Attractions

The Cholistan Desert offers a blend of history, culture, and adventure:

  • Derawar Fort: A massive square fort built in the 9th century, restored by Nawabs of Bahawalpur.
  • Annual Jeep Rally: A motorsport event showcasing adventure tourism in the desert.
  • Nomadic Lifestyle: Traditional camel herding and handicrafts by desert dwellers.
  • Handicrafts: Famous for camel-skin products, weaving, and traditional embroidery.
  • Cultural Festivals: Celebrations with folk music, dance, and local food.
